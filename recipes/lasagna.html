Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian pasta dish. It's made with stacked layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, fillings, and sauces, then baked. It's a popular comfort food worldwide with a rich history and numerous variations.

Main Components

Noodles: Wide, flat pasta sheets form the dish's base. Modern recipes often use no-boil noodles, but traditionally, noodles are boiled al dente.

Sauce: Typically, a rich meat sauce like Bolognese or a simple marinara sauce is used. This often includes tomatoes (crushed, sauce, and paste) for flavor. Some variations also incorporate béchamel (a creamy white sauce) for added richness.

Cheese: A blend of cheeses like ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan is commonly used to create creamy, melty layers.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic cloves
  • Marinara sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Cottage cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Paremsan cheese
  • Egg
  • Parsley
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Salt and pepper
  • Dried thyme
  • Sugar
  • Olive oil
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce
    • Brown the ground beef (and sausage, if using) in a large skillet or pot.
    • Drain any excess fat.
    • Sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
    • Add the marinara sauce, tomato paste, and other desired seasonings.
    • Simmer the sauce for a period to allow flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken.
  2. Cook the Lasagna Noodles (if not using no-boil)
    • Boil the noodles in a large pot of salted water until al dente.
    • Drain the noodles and rinse them with cold water to prevent sticking.
    • Lay the cooled noodles flat on a surface like wax or parchment paper.
  3. Prepare the Ricotta Mixture
    • In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, Parmesan cheese, and any other desired seasonings like parsley.
  4. Assemble the Lasagna
    • Preheat your oven to the temperature specified in your recipe (commonly around 375°F or 190°C).
    • Lightly grease your baking dish.
    • Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of the dish.
    • Arrange a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce, overlapping slightly if necessary.
    • Spread a portion of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
    • Top the ricotta mixture with a layer of meat sauce.
    • Add a layer of mozzarella cheese slices or shredded mozzarella.
    • Repeat the layering process, building several layers of noodles, ricotta, sauce, and mozzarella.
    • Finish with a top layer of noodles, sauce, and remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
    • If using no-boil noodles and your sauce is thick, consider adding a little water around the edges of the pan.
  5. Bake the Lasagna
    • Cover the baking dish with foil. To prevent the cheese from sticking, you can spray the underside of the foil with cooking spray or tent it.
    • Bake for the time recommended in your recipe (often around 30-45 minutes).
    • Remove the foil and continue baking until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the top is golden brown.
    • You can broil for a few minutes at the end for an extra browned and bubbly top, if desired.
  6. Rest and Serve
    • Let the lasagna rest for at least 15 minutes before cutting and serving. This allows the layers to set and makes it easier to slice.
