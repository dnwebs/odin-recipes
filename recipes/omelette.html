Description

An omelette (or omelet in American English) is a dish made from beaten eggs cooked in a pan. The main ingredients are eggs and butter or oil for frying. It is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed plain or with various fillings.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 large eggs
  • Pinch of salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, Gruyère, Swiss, etc.), optional
  • Optional fillings (cooked vegetables, meats, or herbs), see suggestions below

Instructions

  1. Beat the eggs vigorously with a fork until fully combined and seasoned with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat, add butter, and swirl to coat. Pour in the eggs and swirl to distribute.
  3. Cover and cook until the eggs begin to set. Use a spatula to gently push cooked edges towards the center, letting uncooked egg flow underneath until mostly set.
  4. Flip the omelette, turn off the heat, add cheese and toppings to one half.
  5. Fold the other half over the fillings, slide onto a plate, and serve. Garnish as desired.

Omelette Filling Suggestions

  • Vegetables: sautéed mushrooms, spinach, diced tomatoes, caramelized onions, etc.
  • Meats: cooked crumbled sausage, bacon, ham, smoked salmon, etc.
  • Cheeses: Cheddar, Gruyère, Swiss, American, gouda, etc.
  • Herbs: Chives, dill, parsley

Tips for Success

  • Avoid overcooking; the omelette is done when set but still moist.
  • Use a nonstick skillet with sloping sides.
  • Limit fillings to no more than 1/2 cup for a 2-3 egg omelette.
  • Prepare fillings before starting.
  • For a French omelette, use medium-low heat, stir gently while cooking, and roll instead of flipping.
